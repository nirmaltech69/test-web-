What Should You Do If You Have Water In Your Home Right Now?

If you’ve discovered water damage in your home, taking immediate action is essential to reduce damage, prevent mold growth, and protect your property. Even a small amount of standing water can quickly seep into walls, flooring, and furniture, leading to structural issues and health hazards. Follow these steps to minimize damage and restore your home safely.

1. Identify and Stop the Water Source

The first step in handling water damage is to identify where the water is coming from. If the flooding is caused by a burst pipe, leaking appliance, or plumbing failure, locate your main water shut-off valve and turn it off to prevent further flooding. If the water damage is due to an external source such as heavy rainfall, storm flooding, or a sewage backup, try to redirect the water away from your home if possible.

2. Turn Off Electricity in Affected Areas

Water and electricity pose a significant hazard when combined. If water has reached electrical outlets, appliances, or wiring, it’s critical to turn off power to the affected areas immediately. Locate your breaker panel and shut off the electricity to avoid electrical shocks, short circuits, or fire hazards. If you are unsure whether it is safe to do so, wait for a professional to assess the situation.

3. Move Valuables and Protect Furniture

To prevent further damage, move essential items such as furniture, electronics, and important documents to a dry and safe area. If you are unable to move heavy furniture, placing aluminum foil or wooden blocks under furniture legs can help reduce contact with water and prevent permanent damage. Removing area rugs and wet upholstery can also help prevent water from seeping deeper into carpets and floors.

4. Extract Standing Water and Begin Drying

If the water is minimal, you can start by using mops, towels, or a wet-dry vacuum to remove as much water as possible. However, for significant water damage, professional water extraction is necessary. Standing water quickly soaks into walls, floors, and baseboards, leading to structural weakening and mold growth within 24-48 hours. Dehumidifiers and fans can help with initial drying, but professional industrial-grade air movers and dehumidifiers are required for a thorough dry-out process.

How to Prevent Water Damage?

Preventing water damage requires regular maintenance, quick action on leaks, and proper home protection measures. Here are key steps to safeguard your property:

  1. Inspect Plumbing Regularly – Check for leaky pipes, dripping faucets, and worn-out hoses on appliances like dishwashers and washing machines. Replace damaged components immediately.

  2. Maintain Your Roof and Gutters – Clean gutters regularly to prevent water buildup and roof leaks. Inspect your roof for missing shingles, cracks, or weak spots that could allow water intrusion.

  3. Seal Windows and Doors – Ensure window and door seals are watertight to prevent leaks during heavy rain. Use caulking or weather stripping as needed.

  4. Install a Sump Pump and Water Sensors – A sump pump helps prevent basement flooding, while water leak detectors can alert you to leaks before they cause serious damage.

  5. Monitor Water Pressure and Shut-Off Valve – High water pressure can stress pipes, increasing the risk of burst pipes. Know where your main shut-off valve is located so you can stop water flow in an emergency.

  6. Inspect Your Water Heater – A failing water heater can leak or burst, causing significant damage. Look for signs of rust, leaks, or unusual noises and schedule maintenance as needed.

  7. Prevent Frozen Pipes in Cold Weather – Insulate pipes and keep cabinets open during freezing temperatures to allow warm air to circulate. Running a small trickle of water can prevent pipes from freezing and bursting.

By taking these preventative measures, you can reduce the risk of costly water damage and keep your home safe. If you ever experience a water emergency, BIONIC Emergency Services is available 24/7 to respond quickly and restore your property.
